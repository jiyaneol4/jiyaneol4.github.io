Abstract

This journal documents an independent qualitative research project examining how individuals describe using ChatGPT and comparable AI chatbots as a substitute for, or supplement to, conventional therapy. The project is, at the time of writing, in its earliest stage: reading, thinking, and beginning to identify where a genuine gap in existing knowledge might sit, rather than starting from a predetermined research question. This document will be revised and re-dated as the work progresses, and is published openly in the interest of doing the process honestly, in public, rather than only presenting a finished account in retrospect.

Background: Why I Want to Do This

Section status: drafted

Over the past several years, a pattern has become harder to ignore. Across Reddit threads, comment sections, and informal conversation, individuals increasingly describe turning to ChatGPT in moments of emotional distress, often explicitly drawing comparisons to therapeutic relationships. I noticed this first through professional exposure rather than academic curiosity, and the academic curiosity followed afterwards.

I do not yet have a tightly defined research question, and I want to be honest about that rather than retrofit one for the sake of appearing further along than I am. What I have is a genuine fascination with the subject, a research background that makes me confident I can study it properly, and an intention to let an actual literature gap emerge through reading rather than assume one exists before I have looked.

Reflection

I want to be transparent that this project began from professional and personal observation rather than a pre-existing gap identified through systematic review. Across roles in NHS human resources, frontline mental health support work, and academic research, I noticed a recurring pattern in informal accounts and online discourse that I have not yet found addressed with the depth I expected. Whether that impression survives contact with the actual literature is, honestly, an open question, and part of why I am doing this at all.

Reflections

Updated periodically as the project develops
June 2026

I am choosing to publish this journal at an early and admittedly unfinished stage, rather than waiting until I have something more resolved to show. Part of the reason is accountability: making the project public makes it harder to quietly let it drift. Part of it is a genuine belief that the process of doing research, including the uncertain and unglamorous parts, is worth documenting honestly rather than only presenting a tidy account once it is over.

I do not yet know what I will find, or even exactly what question I will end up asking. I am trying, deliberately, to let the gap in the literature reveal itself through reading, rather than deciding in advance what I expect to be missing.
