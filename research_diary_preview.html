FIELD NOTE / ENTRY 001

I do not yet know what I am going to find. For the next few months I am reading, coding, and sitting with public posts from people who describe using ChatGPT the way they might once have used a therapist.

This notebook is where I will think out loud while I do it: the methodology, the messy middle, the moments the data surprises me.

No primary interviews. No ethics board needed for this stage. Just careful, transparent work with what people have already chosen to share.

The Project

Why study this at all?

Somewhere in the last two years, a strange and under-discussed shift happened. People started describing ChatGPT not as a tool, but as something closer to a confidant. Reddit threads on r/ChatGPT, r/therapy, and r/mentalhealth are full of posts that read less like product reviews and more like testimony: relief, dependency, grief that it isn't a real person, gratitude that it is not.

I want to understand this properly rather than react to a headline about it. I am a recent psychology graduate with a research background and, for the first time, no deadline or supervisor assigning the question. So I am asking it myself.

01

Data source

Public posts from mental health and AI-related subreddits. No primary interviews, no new human subjects data collected.

02

Method

Reflexive thematic analysis, coded by hand, themes built and revised openly as the project develops.

03

Where it lands

Full paper to be published on PsyArXiv. This site documents the process alongside it, in real time.
