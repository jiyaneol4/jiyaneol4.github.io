Starting point: what I'm fascinated by, and why I want to do this on my own

I want to use this first entry to be honest about something that took me a little while to admit properly: I am not doing this because anyone asked me to, and I think that is exactly why I want to do it.

Almost everything I have researched until now has happened inside a structure someone else built. My dissertation had a supervisor and a deadline. My research work at Loughborough's Department of Psychology was part of a funded project with academics already attached to it. Even the most independent-feeling parts of my degree were still, ultimately, assessed, marked, and bounded by someone else's framework. I do not say that as a complaint. I think that structure taught me how to do research properly: how to be rigorous, how to think about ethics, how to actually finish something. But it also means I have never yet had the experience of choosing a question entirely for myself, with no one waiting on the other end of it.

This is that. And I want to be honest that it is a slightly strange feeling. There is no supervisor to tell me whether reflexive thematic analysis is the right call, or whether I am even asking the right question yet. No department deciding whether this is worth doing at all. Just my own judgement, which I trust more than I expected to, and a genuine, slightly stubborn fascination with a question I cannot quite put down.

I think real research, the kind that produces something true rather than something merely correct, requires this at some point: a question that exists because you actually want the answer, not because it was assigned to you. I have spent the past few years building the skills, the methodology, the ethical instincts, to do this properly. This is the first time I am pointing all of that at something that is entirely mine, from the first reading to whatever it eventually becomes.

A note on independence

I think there is a meaningful difference between doing research because you have been given a question, and doing research because a question has gotten hold of you. I am not sure yet which category this fully belongs to, but it feels much closer to the second. That distinction is, I suspect, part of why I am writing this journal at all rather than quietly reading in private. If this is going to be mine, I want to actually own it publicly too.

I do not know yet exactly where the gap in the existing literature sits. I am reading to find that out rather than assuming I already know, which is its own kind of discipline I am having to practise. But I wanted this first entry to be less about the topic itself, and more about why doing this independently matters to me. I think it is the most honest place to start, and everything that follows in this journal will build from here.
